Erich Maria Remarque

One of the most influential German writers of XX century.

Timeline of Erich Maria Remarque's life:

  • 1898 - Born in Osnabrück, Germany.
  • 1916 - Publication of "Von den Freuden und Mühen der Jugendwehr". Conscription into the Army.
  • 1917 - Transfer to the Western Front. Wounded by grenade shrapnel and sent to the hospital. Mother dies of cancer while Remarque is recovering at the hospital.
  • 1919 - Began teaching at an elementary school in Lohne.
  • 1920 - Stops teaching at schools, does odd jobs at Osnabrück.
  • 1925 - Moves to Berlin and becomes editor of Sport Im Bild. First marriage to Ilse Jutta “Jeanne” Zambona.
  • 1928 - Publishes his second novel "Station Am Horizont" through Sport Im Bild.
  • 1929 - Publishes one of his most famous books, "Im Westen nichts Neues".
  • 1930 - "All Quiet on the Western Front" gets a movie adaptation. Divorce with Jutta Zambona.
  • 1933 - Books are banned by the rising Nazi party for negative portrayal of war. Moved from Switzerland to Germany.
  • 1938 - Remaried his ex-wife Jutta Zambona.
  • 1939 - Published "Liebe deinen Nächsten". Moved to the United States with his wife.
  • 1946 - Published "Arc de Triomphe".
  • 1948 - Returned to Switzerland.
  • 1957 - Divorced with Jutta Zambona for the second time.
  • 1958 - Married the actress Paulette Goddard.
  • 1970 - Died at the age of 72 in Locarno, Switzerland.

“Life did not intend to make us perfect. Whoever is perfect belongs in a museum.”

--Erich Maria Remarque
